03Target Audience

Primary: Founders & Aspiring Entrepreneurs

People with capital (or access to it) and ambition, but no methodology. They have ideas ‚Äî lots of them, often contradictory. They've been told to "just build it" but don't know what "it" actually is. They need structure without bureaucracy, methodology without MBA jargon.

üí°
The First-Timer
Has money, has an idea, has never built software. Doesn't know they need requirements. Thinks "I want Uber for X" is a spec.
üîÑ
The Serial Pivoter
Changes direction every meeting. New feature every week. Can't articulate a consistent vision because they haven't been forced to confront contradictions.
üí∞
The Funded Founder
Has raised money or bootstrapping with revenue. Needs to translate vision into something a team (or AI agents) can actually execute.

Secondary (Future): Engineering & Product Teams

Once a product is hatched, the ongoing feedback loop ‚Äî bug reports, feature requests, user feedback ‚Äî flows through Kurnik too. Engineering directors and PMs use it to manage the living product. But this is Phase 2+ audience.

Decision

Day one brand voice speaks to founders. Approachable, warm, confident. Not technical jargon. Not enterprise sales language. Think: "Your ideas are valuable. Let's make sense of them together."

04Brand Personality

Voice Profile: Warm & Confident

Notion meets Stripe. Approachable but credible. Kurnik doesn't talk down to you for having messy ideas ‚Äî messy ideas are the raw material. But it also doesn't pretend chaos is fine. It's the warm, honest advisor who says "I see what you're going for ‚Äî let me show you the contradictions."

Symbol Direction: Abstract Rooster

Decision

Strong presence ‚Äî the rooster/chicken is the main logo element, but stylized and abstract. Not a literal chicken illustration. Think geometric, minimal, recognizable at 16px. The silhouette should read as "rooster" without being a clip art chicken.
